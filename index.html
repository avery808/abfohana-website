My Story

I Came From Nothing.
I Built It Anyway.

I was born to a 15-year-old mother in Hawaii and raised in poverty by my aunt Pamila — a woman who chose me when she didn't have to. We didn't have money. We had work ethic, love, and the ocean.

I joined the Army at 18 because it was the only door open. Twenty years later I retired as a Master Sergeant (E-8) after two combat deployments, 17 concussions, PTSD, and TBI. They gave me 100% disability. I gave myself a new mission.

While recovering, I discovered something: the same systems that build wealth — real estate, passive income, smart structuring — were available to anyone who knew how to use them. Most people just never got taught.

That's what ABF Ohana is. Ohana means family in Hawaiian. My clients aren't customers. They're family. And family doesn't let family stay broke.
